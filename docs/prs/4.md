# PR: Issue 4 - Cards, effects, and the turn engine

Branch: `4-cards-effects-and-turn-engine`. Source: `docs/plans/ascent.md`.

## Summary

Build the combat loop: cards that carry effects, a single resolver that applies
them, and a turn engine over energy, hand, draw and discard piles, and seeded
enemy intents. This is the playable heart of the game; after this issue a combat
can be driven to a win or a loss entirely from the headless core.

Cards are frozen data (name, energy cost, a tuple of effects). Effects are frozen
data too (`Damage`, `GainBlock`); a single `resolve` function is the only place
they mutate state, so all damage and block bookkeeping stays in one spot. Enemies
carry a set of possible moves and a telegraphed intent chosen from the seeded
`enemy_ai` stream. The turn engine spends energy to play cards, ends the turn by
resolving enemy intents against the player, then starts a fresh player turn
(block cleared, energy refilled, hand redrawn), reshuffling the discard into the
draw pile via the seeded `shuffle` stream when the draw pile empties.

## How it was tested

Six tests, kept small: one for effect resolution and five for the turn engine
(playing a card, ending the turn, reshuffle-on-empty, win, and lose).

## Cycle 2: GREEN

**Implementation**

- `intents.py`: `IntentKind` (currently just `ATTACK`) and the frozen `Intent`
  with an `attack` convenience constructor.
- `effects.py`: frozen `Damage` and `GainBlock`, the `Effect` union, and
  `resolve`. `resolve` matches on the effect type and is the single mutation
  site; `Damage` requires a target and raises without one, and a `case _`
  guarded by `assert_never` keeps the union exhaustive as effects are added.
- `cards.py`: the frozen `Card` (name, cost, tuple of effects).
- `entities.py`: `EnemyCombatant` extends `Combatant` with a move set and a
  telegraphed intent.
- `combat.py`: `CombatState`. `play_card` checks the card is in hand and
  affordable, spends energy, resolves effects against the first living enemy,
  and discards the card. `end_turn` resolves each living enemy's intent, then
  starts a fresh player turn (block cleared, energy refilled, hand redrawn).
  `draw` reshuffles the discard through the seeded `shuffle` stream when the draw
  pile empties, and stops if nothing is left. Intents are telegraphed from the
  seeded `enemy_ai` stream.

Targeting is implicit for the slice (damage hits the first living enemy, block
shields the player). Status effects such as Vulnerable and Weak are not
implemented yet.
